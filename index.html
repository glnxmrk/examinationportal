Subject: Community Engagement, Solidarity and Citizenship

1. According to the Office of the United Nations High Commissioner for Human Rights, human rights are:
Choices: Granted only to citizens of a country., Rights that can be revoked by the state., Inherent to all of us, regardless of nationality, sex, or origin., Only applicable during times of peace. | Ans: Inherent to all of us, regardless of nationality, sex, or origin.
2. This core value is defined as the view that everyone deserves equal economic, political, and social rights and opportunities.
Choices: Human Rights, Social Justice, Gender Equality, Participatory Development | Ans: Social Justice
3. What term refers to promoting a cause and enabling marginalized groups to know and exercise their rights to a larger sphere?
Choices: Empowerment, Advocacy, Participation, Equity | Ans: Advocacy
4. A community project aims to build a new health center. The project planners ensure that local residents are involved in every step, from planning to implementation, believing that this will make the project more sustainable. This approach BEST exemplifies:
Choices: Gender Equality, Social Justice, Participatory Development, Advocacy | Ans: Participatory Development
5. Which class of rights is possessed by every citizen without being granted by the state and cannot be repealed by human laws, such as the right to life?
Choices: Constitutional Rights, Statutory Rights, Natural Rights, Political Rights | Ans: Natural Rights
6. The right to suffrage and the right to run for public office are examples of:
Choices: Civil rights, Political rights, Social and economic rights, Natural rights | Ans: Political rights
7. Article III, Section I of the 1987 Philippine Constitution states that no person shall be deprived of life, liberty, or property without:
Choices: a warrant of arrest., due process of law., a congressional hearing., the approval of the President. | Ans: due process of law.
8. A local ordinance is passed that requires a specific permit for small sari-sari stores, a requirement not applied to larger supermarkets. This could be seen as a violation of which principle?
Choices: The right to life, The right to property and equal protection of the laws, The right to suffrage, The right to form associations | Ans: The right to property and equal protection of the laws
9. According to the discussion on social justice, it takes place if there are equal rights, equal opportunity, and:
Choices: equal wealth for all., equal treatment for every member of the community., the same number of leaders from each group., the absence of any laws. | Ans: equal treatment for every member of the community.
10. A school has only one basketball court. The men's basketball team uses it every day after school because they arrive first, leaving no time for the women's team to practice. This situation is an issue of:
Choices: human rights violation., lack of advocacy., access and participation., empowerment. | Ans: access and participation.
11. The four essential goals for achieving social justice are human rights, access, participation, and:
Choices: Equity, Advocacy, Empowerment, Development | Ans: Equity
12. Gender equality is 'achieved when men and women have equal rights, freedoms, conditions, and opportunities.' (Hamelin 2012, 3). This definition emphasizes:
Choices: that men and women are the same., that women should be given more rights to compensate for the past., the equal value and treatment of all genders., that gender is not an important factor in society. | Ans: the equal value and treatment of all genders.
13. A company ensures that its hiring process does not discriminate against women and that it provides the same training opportunities to all employees regardless of gender. This is an example of promoting:
Choices: Gender Equity, Gender Equality, Gender Discrimination, Women Empowerment | Ans: Gender Equality
14. What is the term for 'any distinction, exclusion, or restriction made on the basis of sex which has the effect or purpose of impairing or nullifying the recognition, enjoyment, or exercise by women' (UN CEDAW)?
Choices: Gender Equality, Gender Equity, Gender Discrimination, Gender Stereotyping | Ans: Gender Discrimination
15. Empowerment is achieved when people gain:
Choices: greater influence and control over their own life and environment., more material wealth., popularity in their community., the ability to lead others without question. | Ans: greater influence and control over their own life and environment.
16. A group of residents in a flood-prone area organizes themselves, learns about the local disaster risk reduction plan, and successfully lobbies the barangay captain to include their sitio in the early warning system. This scenario is a direct example of:
Choices: Social Justice, Empowerment, Gender Equality, Participatory Development | Ans: Empowerment
17. Policy advocacy aims to affect change by directly influencing:
Choices: the general public's behavior., government institutions and legislation., private corporations only., international media. | Ans: government institutions and legislation.
18. An organization launches a massive social media campaign to educate the public about the dangers of single-use plastics and to encourage people to switch to reusable bags. This is an example of:
Choices: Policy Advocacy, Public Advocacy, Lobbying, Program Development | Ans: Public Advocacy
19. This type of empowerment refers to the result of a community that can manage its resources and can exercise control over the factors that affect them.
Choices: Individual Empowerment, Psychological Empowerment, Organizational Empowerment, Community Empowerment | Ans: Community Empowerment
20. Participatory development is a process that aims to give every member of the population, particularly the poor, marginalized, and vulnerable members, a part in:
Choices: national elections., initiatives and projects implemented for them., the profit of local businesses., creating the national budget. | Ans: initiatives and projects implemented for them.
21. Why is 'active participation' crucial for the success of a community project?
Choices: It ensures that the project leaders have all the power., It involves co-determining and power-sharing among community members, leading to a greater sense of ownership and sustainability., It allows the community to rely entirely on external funders., It speeds up the project by limiting input from community members. | Ans: It involves co-determining and power-sharing among community members, leading to a greater sense of ownership and sustainability.
22. Which of the following is NOT listed as an importance of participation?
Choices: It results in developmental efficiency., It becomes a means of gaining access and control over resources., It guarantees immediate financial profit for all members., It promotes well-being as a result of community empowerment. | Ans: It guarantees immediate financial profit for all members.
23. A community is divided by factions. To promote participation in a new livelihood program, the community leaders should FIRST:
Choices: ignore the factions and proceed with the plan., choose one faction to lead the project., come up with programs that favor the whole community to encourage everyone to work together., ask the government to intervene and force the groups to participate. | Ans: come up with programs that favor the whole community to encourage everyone to work together.
24. What is the main principle behind participatory development?
Choices: Development is faster when led by external experts., Without the commitment, creativity, and efforts of the people, development may take longer to accomplish., Only the government should be responsible for development., The private sector is the main driver of development. | Ans: Without the commitment, creativity, and efforts of the people, development may take longer to accomplish.
25. This involves a number of responsibilities by both the community and the organization or local group with which it has made an agreement to achieve a common goal.
Choices: Needs Assessment, Community Profiling, Partnership Building, Leadership Development | Ans: Partnership Building
26. Which of the following is a responsibility in partnership building?
Choices: Holding regular meetings and monitoring program progress., Competing with the partner group for resources., Keeping financial records a secret., Avoiding communication with community members. | Ans: Holding regular meetings and monitoring program progress.
27. A local farmers' cooperative wants to partner with an NGO to improve their irrigation system. According to the process of partnership building, what should be their FIRST step?
Choices: Immediately start building the irrigation system., Monitor the progress of the project., Connect with the leaders of the NGO to ensure their goals are aligned., Liquidate their finances. | Ans: Connect with the leaders of the NGO to ensure their goals are aligned.
28. The method of partnership building that focuses on changing or augmenting public policies or submitting legislations is called:
Choices: Promotion, Program Development, Advocacy, Collaboration | Ans: Advocacy
29. A community profile is a collection of information that describes a community's:
Choices: land uses, natural environment, economy, cultural traditions, and history., only the number of people living in it., future plans without any data from the past., problems and weaknesses only. | Ans: land uses, natural environment, economy, cultural traditions, and history.
30. A community is applying for a grant from a national government agency to build a library. To support their application, they need to present data on the number of students, current literacy rates, and existing educational facilities. This information would be BEST presented in a:
Choices: Leadership Development Plan., Community Profile., Needs Assessment Form., Partnership Agreement. | Ans: Community Profile.
31. Which is the FIRST step in doing a community profile?
Choices: Summarize findings., Develop the community profile., Establish a general picture of the community., Consult with community leaders and members. | Ans: Consult with community leaders and members.
32. In constructing a community profile form, which theme of information includes demographics, health, and education?
Choices: Community's environment, Social aspects of the community, Community's culture, Community's economy | Ans: Social aspects of the community
33. A community leader wants to validate the information gathered from a survey about local health issues. Which source of information would be MOST appropriate for this step?
Choices: A national census report from 10 years ago., The community members themselves through interviews or focus groups., A textbook on public health., The mayor's campaign promises. | Ans: The community members themselves through interviews or focus groups.
34. Needs assessment is a process of establishing and addressing needs or 'gaps' between:
Choices: the community's leaders and its members., the community's current conditions and its ideal conditions or 'wants'., two different communities., the government's plan and the NGO's plan. | Ans: the community's current conditions and its ideal conditions or 'wants'.
35. A barangay has a high unemployment rate. They conduct a survey and find that while many adults are willing to work, they lack specific skills for jobs available in the nearby city. In a needs assessment, this lack of skills represents the:
Choices: desired outcome., current condition., need or gap., solution. | Ans: need or gap.
36. After collecting data for a needs assessment, what is the next logical step?
Choices: Immediately start a project., Summarize the data and create the needs assessment report., Disband the planning team., Present the raw, unanalyzed data to the whole community. | Ans: Summarize the data and create the needs assessment report.
37. When formulating a needs assessment form, why is it important to consider the language of the respondents and avoid leading questions?
Choices: To make the form look more professional., To ensure the data collected is accurate, honest, and relevant., To save time and money on printing., To make it easier for the planning team to analyze. | Ans: To ensure the data collected is accurate, honest, and relevant.
38. Leadership development expands the capacity of individuals to perform leadership roles within groups. A core group of leaders is needed to:
Choices: make all decisions without consulting others., motivate members to participate in initiatives for the community., compete with other communities., ensure that only they receive the benefits of projects. | Ans: motivate members to participate in initiatives for the community.
39. Which skill is essential for a core group of leaders to exemplify?
Choices: Keeping all information secret., Effective communication skills., Ignoring feedback from members., Avoiding new ideas. | Ans: Effective communication skills.
40. A community project leader gathers her team, presents the problem of a lack of a youth center, asks for everyone's ideas, and together they weigh the pros and cons of building it in different locations before deciding. This leader is effectively using the:
Choices: process of delegation., decision-making process., laissez-faire approach., needs assessment form. | Ans: decision-making process.
41. In the decision-making process, what step comes immediately after gathering relevant information?
Choices: Take action., Identify the alternatives., Weigh the evidence., Review the decision. | Ans: Identify the alternatives.
42. A seasoned barangay captain spends time mentoring a young SK chairperson, teaching her how to manage the youth council's funds and navigate local politics. This is an example of which leadership development method?
Choices: Leadership Development Programs, Real-Time Experiences and On-the-Job Training, Mentoring and Coaching Initiatives, Regular Feedback | Ans: Mentoring and Coaching Initiatives
43. A leader who informs members of a common goal and vision but maintains a clear division between themselves and the members is practicing what type of leadership?
Choices: Participative Leadership, Authoritarian Leadership, Laissez-Faire Leadership, Servant Leadership | Ans: Authoritarian Leadership
44. A 'servant leader' is best described as one who:
Choices: makes all the decisions for the group., leads from behind and allows the focus to be on community members., delegates all tasks and does not get involved., focuses only on their personal power and publicity. | Ans: leads from behind and allows the focus to be on community members.
45. In a community organization, the leader trusts her members completely and allows them to make their own decisions and set their own deadlines for a project. This is a characteristic of:
Choices: Transformational Leadership, Authoritarian Leadership, Laissez-Faire Leadership, Servant Leadership | Ans: Laissez-Faire Leadership
46. The approach to partnership building that involves stakeholder consultation and is usually the best model for a local government unit sustainability plan is:
Choices: Collaboration, Participation, Advocacy, Promotion | Ans: Participation
47. An organization uses social media campaigns, community fairs, and informational materials to raise public awareness about the importance of mangrove reforestation. This method of partnership building is called:
Choices: Advocacy, Promotion, Program Development, Lobbying | Ans: Promotion
48. Which of the following is a weakness or challenge in doing a community profile?
Choices: It encourages a broader ideology about the community., It can be an opportunity to develop relationships., Communities are complex, and managing the vast amount of information can be difficult., It highlights the gaps in understanding a community. | Ans: Communities are complex, and managing the vast amount of information can be difficult.
49. A community successfully lobbied the city council to pass an ordinance protecting a local watershed. This is a direct result of effective:
Choices: community profiling., needs assessment., partnership building and advocacy., leadership development programs. | Ans: partnership building and advocacy.
50. The primary purpose of reviewing a decision and its consequences in the decision-making process is to:
Choices: assign blame if the decision was wrong., consider the results and evaluate if it resolved the identified need., gather more information for a new decision., create a report for the government. | Ans: consider the results and evaluate if it resolved the identified need.
