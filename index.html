Subject: Practical Research I

1. Which of the following correctly defines 'inquiry' in research?üñº Image
Choices: A process of validating existing theories without questioning, A learning process that motivates one to obtain knowledge by asking questions, A method of gathering numerical data only, The final step in writing a research paper | Ans: A learning process that motivates one to obtain knowledge by asking questions
2. What is the main purpose of a 'Narrative Hook' in the Background of the Study?
Choices: To present the methodology, To list the research questions, To draw the reader into the study, To state the scope and delimitations | Ans: To draw the reader into the study
3. Which research type aims to apply findings to societal problems or issues?
Choices: Pure Research, Exploratory Research, Applied Research, Descriptive Research | Ans: Applied Research
4. What is the correct order of sections in Chapter 1 of a research paper?
Choices: Significance, Background, Statement of the Problem, Scope, Background, Statement of the Problem, Significance, Scope, Scope, Background, Significance, Statement of the Problem, Background, Scope, Significance, Statement of the Problem | Ans: Background, Statement of the Problem, Significance, Scope
5. Which of the following is a characteristic of Qualitative Research?
Choices: Uses only numerical data, Requires controlled variables, Abounds with words and visuals, Avoids human interpretation | Ans: Abounds with words and visuals
6. What does 'Scope and Delimitation' of a study primarily define?
Choices: The theoretical framework, The boundaries and weaknesses of the study, The data analysis procedure, The list of references | Ans: The boundaries and weaknesses of the study
7. In research, what is a 'Purpose Statement'?
Choices: A broad subject matter, The major intent or objective of the study, A list of variables, The final conclusion | Ans: The major intent or objective of the study
8. Which of the following is an example of a Secondary Data source?
Choices: Personal interviews, Survey results, Government publications, Laboratory experiments | Ans: Government publications
9. What is the main goal of Ethnography as a qualitative research type?
Choices: To examine historical documents, To study a particular cultural group's lifestyle, To measure numerical relationships, To test a new theory | Ans: To study a particular cultural group's lifestyle
10. Which term refers to the 'general educational issue or concern addressed in research'?
Choices: Research Question, Purpose Statement, Research Problem, Topic | Ans: Research Problem
11. What is a key guideline in framing a research title?
Choices: It must be more than 20 words, It must explicitly state the research design, It must list key variables, It must include the researcher's name | Ans: It must list key variables
12. Which approach combines scientific and naturalistic methods?
Choices: Pure Approach, Triangulation Approach, Positive Approach, Deductive Approach | Ans: Triangulation Approach
13. Which section of the research paper states who will benefit from the study?
Choices: Background of the Study, Statement of the Problem, Significance of the Study, Scope and Delimitation | Ans: Significance of the Study
14. What type of research 'defines or gives a portrayal of a person, thing, or event'?
Choices: Correlational, Explanatory, Descriptive, Action | Ans: Descriptive
15. Which of the following is NOT a good source of researchable problems?
Choices: Personal fantasies without evidence, Previous research recommendations, National trends, Real-life student issues | Ans: Personal fantasies without evidence
16. A study aims to determine the relationship between sleep duration and academic performance of SHS students. Which research type is most appropriate?
Choices: Descriptive, Correlational, Historical, Phenomenology | Ans: Correlational
17. Which research title is most appropriately framed according to the standards discussed?
Choices: 'A Study on Social Media', 'The Effect of Facebook Usage on Attention Span of Grade 11 Students', 'How Social Media is Bad for Students: A Qualitative Inquiry', 'An Investigation into the Relationship Between Two Variables' | Ans: 'The Effect of Facebook Usage on Attention Span of Grade 11 Students'
18. In a study on 'Math Anxiety and Problem-Solving Skills,' which part of Chapter 1 would explain why past studies show conflicting results?
Choices: Significance of the Study, Background of the Study, Statement of the Problem, Scope and Delimitation | Ans: Background of the Study
19. A researcher writes: 'This study aims to explore the lived experiences of teenage mothers.' Which qualitative research type is this?
Choices: Case Study, Ethnography, Phenomenology, Grounded Theory | Ans: Phenomenology
20. Which of the following is the BEST example of a Research Question derived from the Purpose Statement: 'To identify factors affecting low grades in Mathematics'?
Choices: What is Mathematics?, How many students fail Math?, What are the factors affecting low grades in Mathematics among SHS students?, Why do students dislike Math? | Ans: What are the factors affecting low grades in Mathematics among SHS students?
21. If a study uses both surveys and interviews to examine bullying, which research approach is being used?
Choices: Scientific Approach, Naturalistic Approach, Triangulation Approach, Pure Approach | Ans: Triangulation Approach
22. Which of the following topics is MOST appropriate for a qualitative case study?
Choices: The GDP of the Philippines from 2000-2020, Coping Mechanisms of a Student with Dyslexia, The Effect of Light on Plant Growth, A Nationwide Survey on Voting Preferences | Ans: Coping Mechanisms of a Student with Dyslexia
23. What is the main weakness of a study that uses only self-reported questionnaires for data on social media addiction?
Choices: Too broad scope, Possible subjectivity and reliability issues, Lack of literature support, Too few respondents | Ans: Possible subjectivity and reliability issues
24. Which Statement of the Problem is correctly phrased?
Choices: 'This study aims to prove that social media is harmful.', 'This study generally aims to determine the relationship between study habits and grades.', 'This study will answer everything about learning.', 'This study seeks to find out if students are happy.' | Ans: 'This study generally aims to determine the relationship between study habits and grades.'
25. Given the topic 'Online Learning Fatigue,' which Background of the Study opening is the strongest Narrative Hook?
Choices: 'Online learning is a method of education.', 'According to DepEd, 80% of students reported mental exhaustion after online classes.', 'This study is about online learning.', 'Many students use computers.' | Ans: 'According to DepEd, 80% of students reported mental exhaustion after online classes.'
26. A study on 'Leadership Styles in Student Organizations' uses interviews, observations, and document review. Which qualitative research type does this resemble?
Choices: Historical Analysis, Content Analysis, Case Study, Grounded Theory | Ans: Case Study
27. Which of the following is a delimitation?
Choices: The study is conducted at DLSAU., The study uses random sampling., The study does not include junior high school students., The study aims to improve teaching strategies. | Ans: The study does not include junior high school students.
28. What is the primary difference between 'Topic' and 'Research Problem'?
Choices: Topic is specific, Research Problem is broad, Topic is broad, Research Problem narrows the topic into an issue, Topic is a question, Research Problem is an answer, There is no difference | Ans: Topic is broad, Research Problem narrows the topic into an issue
29. Which of the following Significance of the Study statements is MOST specific?
Choices: 'This study will help everyone.', 'Teachers may use the findings to design attention-focused activities.', 'The study is significant.', 'It will contribute to knowledge.' | Ans: 'Teachers may use the findings to design attention-focused activities.'
30. If a researcher wants to develop a new theory about student motivation during the pandemic, which qualitative type is best?
Choices: Ethnography, Grounded Theory, Phenomenology, Historical Analysis | Ans: Grounded Theory
31. Why is 'Triangulation Approach' considered advantageous?
Choices: It uses only one data source, It allows viewing the research from multiple perspectives, It is the easiest approach, It avoids human subjectivity | Ans: It allows viewing the research from multiple perspectives
32. Which of the following is a researchable topic?
Choices: The beauty of sunsets, The effectiveness of a new teaching method on student engagement, Why aliens exist, My personal opinion about movies | Ans: The effectiveness of a new teaching method on student engagement
33. In Grounded Theory, when does the theory emerge?
Choices: Before data collection, During data collection and analysis, After writing the conclusion, It is borrowed from past studies | Ans: During data collection and analysis
34. Which is a limitation in a qualitative study?
Choices: The sample size is small, The topic is timely, The respondents are cooperative, The data is plentiful | Ans: The sample size is small
35. Based on the Purpose Statement 'To examine the impact of parental involvement on academic performance,' which research question is correctly formulated?
Choices: What is academic performance?, How does parental involvement affect the academic performance of Grade 12 students?, Are parents important?, How many parents are involved? | Ans: How does parental involvement affect the academic performance of Grade 12 students?
36. What is the main advantage of qualitative research in social settings?
Choices: It provides statistical generalizability, It promotes deep understanding of human behavior in natural contexts, It is quick to conduct, It avoids subjectivity completely | Ans: It promotes deep understanding of human behavior in natural contexts
37. Which section of the paper would address 'Why is there a need to study this problem now?'
Choices: Scope, Background, Significance, Title | Ans: Background
38. A study that examines language use in political speeches is an example of:
Choices: Historical Analysis, Discourse Analysis, Case Study, Ethnography | Ans: Discourse Analysis
39. Which of the following is a good example of a 'clear need for research' narrative hook?
Choices: 'Education is important.', 'Many students use Facebook.', 'School dropout rates have increased by 20% in the last five years.', 'This study is interesting.' | Ans: 'School dropout rates have increased by 20% in the last five years.'
40. From the Research Problem 'Low interest in Science among SHS students,' which is the best research title?
Choices: 'Science Education', 'Factors Affecting Low Interest in Science Among Senior High School Students', 'A Research Study', 'Why Students Hate Science' | Ans: 'Factors Affecting Low Interest in Science Among Senior High School Students'
41. Which type of data is collected through direct observation or interaction?
Choices: Secondary data, Primary data, Historical data, Published data | Ans: Primary data
42. If a study's background cites conflicting findings from previous studies, what is it establishing?
Choices: Scope, Research Gap, Methodology, Data analysis | Ans: Research Gap
43. In John Dewey's theory, what is essential for inquiry?
Choices: Isolated thinking, Connected experiences, Memorization, Standardized testing | Ans: Connected experiences
44. Which of the following topics is TOO BROAD for a student research?
Choices: 'The History of the World', 'Reading Habits of Grade 11 STEM Students at DLSAU', 'Social Media Use and Attention Span in One Class', 'Coping Strategies of Public School Teachers in Manila' | Ans: 'The History of the World'
45. For a study on 'Bullying in SHS,' which is the best example of a specific beneficiary in the Significance section?
Choices: 'All people', 'School counselors may use findings to design anti-bullying programs', 'It will be significant', 'Future researchers' | Ans: 'School counselors may use findings to design anti-bullying programs'
46. What is the main difference between Pure and Applied Research?
Choices: Pure Research is practical, Applied is theoretical, Pure Research increases knowledge, Applied solves societal problems, Pure Research uses numbers, Applied uses words, There is no difference | Ans: Pure Research increases knowledge, Applied solves societal problems
47. Which of the following is NOT a characteristic of research?
Choices: Accuracy, Bias, Objectiveness, Systematic process | Ans: Bias
48. Why is 'Availability of Information' important in choosing a research topic?
Choices: It makes the research easier to complete, It ensures there are sources to support the study, It guarantees publication, It avoids primary data collection | Ans: It ensures there are sources to support the study
49. Which of the following is a strength of qualitative research?
Choices: It is highly objective, It allows statistical generalization, It promotes deep contextual understanding, It is quick to analyze | Ans: It promotes deep contextual understanding
50. Given the Research Question 'How do SHS students perceive online learning?' which qualitative research type is most suitable?
Choices: Historical Analysis, Phenomenology, Content Analysis, Correlational Research | Ans: Phenomenology
