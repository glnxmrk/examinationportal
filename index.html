Subject: Community Engagement, Solidarity and Citizenship

1. Which of the following is NOT a characteristic of a community?
Choices: Definite locality, Legal status, Common sentiments, Group of people | Ans: Legal status
2. Which dimension of a community deals with governance, laws, and authority?
Choices: Social, Economic, Political, Cultural | Ans: Political
3. A community where people live in an agricultural area with low population density is called:
Choices: Urban, Suburban, Rural, Industrial | Ans: Rural
4. Which perspective views a community as a set of interactions with shared meanings?
Choices: Institutional Perspective, Social Construct Perspective, Civil Society Perspective, Grassroots Perspective | Ans: Social Construct Perspective
5. Which of the following is an example of community action?
Choices: Ignoring local issues, Volunteering in a barangay clean-up, Avoiding community meetings, Criticizing neighbors | Ans: Volunteering in a barangay clean-up
6. The sense of responsibility and accountability to one's community is called:
Choices: Citizenship, Solidarity, Engagement, Diversity | Ans: Solidarity
7. Which term refers to the unfair treatment of people based on race, gender, or age?
Choices: Stereotyping, Discrimination, Diversity, Inclusion | Ans: Discrimination
8. The process of working collaboratively with a community through volunteering is known as:
Choices: Socialization, Community Engagement, Leadership, Economic Activity | Ans: Community Engagement
9. Which of the following is a part of the socio-political structure of a community?
Choices: Local businesses, Socialization, Religious festivals, Agricultural products | Ans: Socialization
10. What is the term for a shared benefit received by most or all community members?
Choices: Personal gain, Common good, Individual right, Private interest | Ans: Common good
11. Which of these is a characteristic of a good citizen?
Choices: Being a free rider, Avoiding voting, Promoting welfare of minorities, Ignoring environmental issues | Ans: Promoting welfare of minorities
12. Diversity in a community can be based on all EXCEPT:
Choices: Culture, Uniformity, Gender, Religion | Ans: Uniformity
13. Which perspective compares a community to a living creature with interconnected parts?
Choices: Social Science Perspective, Institutional Perspective, Civil Society Perspective, Grassroots Perspective | Ans: Institutional Perspective
14. The phrase "unity in diversity" emphasizes:
Choices: Exclusion of differences, Inclusion of various backgrounds, Uniform beliefs, Single cultural identity | Ans: Inclusion of various backgrounds
15. Which of the following is an example of an economic structure activity?
Choices: Celebrating a fiesta, Voting for a barangay captain, Running a sari-sari store, Teaching children values | Ans: Running a sari-sari store
16. What does the "Grassroots Level Perspective" focus on?
Choices: Government policies, Individual sense of belonging, Large corporations, International relations | Ans: Individual sense of belonging
17. Which is NOT a facet in forming collective identity?
Choices: Cognitive definition, Emotional investments, Active relationship, Legal documentation | Ans: Legal documentation
18. The term for overgeneralizing beliefs about a group of people is:
Choices: Discrimination, Diversity, Stereotyping, Inclusion | Ans: Stereotyping
19. Which profession encourages individuals to overcome challenges using psychological principles?
Choices: Journalist, Guidance Counselor, Social Worker, Business Owner | Ans: Guidance Counselor
20. What is a community's culture shaped by?
Choices: Only economic activities, History, interaction, and beliefs, Government policies only, Foreign influences only | Ans: History, interaction, and beliefs
21. Which of the following is a benefit of shared identity?
Choices: Increased conflict, Decreased cooperation, Greater sense of unity, Less communication | Ans: Greater sense of unity
22. Which type of community is "highly commercialized and industrialized"?
Choices: Rural, Suburban, Urban, Agricultural | Ans: Urban
23. Who organizes communities and promotes empowerment of marginalized groups?
Choices: Guidance Counselors, Social Workers, Journalists, Teachers | Ans: Social Workers
24. Which of these is a characteristic of a community with "no legal status"?
Choices: It can sue in court, It has legal obligations, It cannot claim legal rights, It is registered with the government | Ans: It cannot claim legal rights
25. The ability of a community to control its activities and resources refers to:
Choices: Social Change, Community Power Relations, Leadership, Economic Policy | Ans: Community Power Relations
26. Which of the following is a context of diversity?
Choices: Uniformity, Gender, Monopoly, Isolation | Ans: Gender
27. Which perspective emphasizes rights, organizations, and activism?
Choices: Institutional Perspective, Civil Society Perspective, Social Science Perspective, Grassroots Perspective | Ans: Civil Society Perspective
28. What does "community dynamics" refer to?
Choices: Static and unchanging structures, Patterns of change and activity, Only economic growth, Government laws only | Ans: Patterns of change and activity
29. Which of the following is an example of a social process within a community?
Choices: Opening a bank, Neighbors talking about local issues, Building a factory, Importing goods | Ans: Neighbors talking about local issues
30. "National identity" is a form of:
Choices: Personal identity, Shared identity, Legal identity, Professional identity | Ans: Shared identity
31. Which of these is a material example of the common good?
Choices: A public park, A private car, A personal belief, A family tradition | Ans: A public park
32. Which of the following is a barrier to recognizing diversity?
Choices: Inclusion, Stereotyping, Acceptance, Respect | Ans: Stereotyping
33. What is the primary focus of the "Social Science Perspective"?
Choices: Individual emotions, Social and political systems, Business profits, Artistic expression | Ans: Social and political systems
34. Which of these is NOT a type of community based on sociological perspective?
Choices: Rural, Urban, Suburban, National | Ans: National
35. A community near a river that relies on fishing shares which characteristic?
Choices: Particular Name, Shared Likeness, No Legal Status, Naturality | Ans: Shared Likeness
36. Which of these professionals provides fair and relevant information to communities?
Choices: Social Worker, Journalist, Guidance Counselor, Barangay Captain | Ans: Journalist
37. The process of learning values and norms of a community is called:
Choices: Discrimination, Socialization, Stereotyping, Engagement | Ans: Socialization
38. What does "cognitive definition" refer to in forming collective identity?
Choices: Emotional bonds, Framework for goals and actions, Legal agreements, Economic plans | Ans: Framework for goals and actions
39. Which of the following is a characteristic of a suburban community?
Choices: Highly industrialized, Agricultural landscape, Medium population density, Very low human interaction | Ans: Medium population density
40. Which term describes alterations in cultural attitudes and social organization?
Choices: Economic Growth, Social Change, Political Campaign, Cultural Stagnation | Ans: Social Change
41. Which of these is an example of a cooperative under economic structure?
Choices: Fast-food chain, Credit union, Private corporation, International bank | Ans: Credit union
42. The statement "A community has no legal rights" refers to which characteristic?
Choices: Definite Locality, No Legal Status, Common Sentiments, Wider Ends | Ans: No Legal Status
43. Which of the following is a cultural structure element?
Choices: Stock market, Religious festival, Tax collection, Traffic laws | Ans: Religious festival
44. What does "emotional investments" mean in collective identity formation?
Choices: Financial contributions, Recognition of shared emotions, Legal agreements, Political campaigns | Ans: Recognition of shared emotions
45. Which of these is an example of community action through citizenship?
Choices: Ignoring local elections, Fulfilling duties like voting, Avoiding neighbors, Criticizing community projects | Ans: Fulfilling duties like voting
46. Which perspective is most concerned with individual sense of belonging beyond formal definitions?
Choices: Institutional, Grassroots, Civil Society, Social Science | Ans: Grassroots
47. Which of these is a factor affecting diversity negatively?
Choices: Inclusion, Discrimination, Acceptance, Unity | Ans: Discrimination
48. What is the main purpose of studying community dynamics?
Choices: To keep communities unchanged, To understand and improve communities, To increase individual profit, To reduce social interaction | Ans: To understand and improve communities
49. Which of the following is a "shared likeness" in a community?
Choices: Different religions, Varied economic status, Common way of earning a living, Diverse political views | Ans: Common way of earning a living
50. Applied Social Sciences aim to:
Choices: Ignore social issues, Address social issues progressively, Promote individual interests only, Support only government policies | Ans: Address social issues progressively
